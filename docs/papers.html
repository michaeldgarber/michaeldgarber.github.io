Papers


This page summarizes a selection of my published research by topic. A complete list of my publications can be found at my Google Scholar profile. I’ve linked to a full text if available. I would happily send a full text of any pay-walled articles by email (hi@michaeldgarber.com).

Effect of the built environment on bicycling

My colleagues and I used the generalized synthetic control method to estimate effects of large-scale bicycle infrastructure projects in Atlanta (including the Atlanta BeltLine) on bicycling.

  • Have paved trails and protected bike lanes led to more bicycling in Atlanta? A generalized synthetic-control analysis
    Michael Garber, Dana Flanders, Kari Watkins, Felipe Lobelo, Michael Kramer, Lauren McCullough
    Epidemiology. 2022. (Video abstract)

Sampling, selection bias, and mobile-device data

We have also aimed to better understand how epidemiologic concepts, including case-control sampling and conditions for no selection bias, can inform the valid use of data generated from mobile devices in research. I am most of proud of this paper, in which we extend case-control sampling principles to show how aggregated smartphone-generated data can be used to estimate the incidence rate ratio in epidemiologic studies.

  • On selection bias in comparison measures of smartphone-generated population mobility: an illustration of no-bias conditions with a commercial data source
    Michael Garber, Katie Labgold, Michael Kramer
    Annals of Epidemiology. 2022.

  • At-risk-measure sampling in case-control studies with aggregated data
    Michael Garber, Lauren McCullough, Steve Mooney, Michael Kramer, Kari Watkins, Felipe Lobelo, Dana Flanders
    Epidemiology. 2021. (full text above and in PubMed Central) (GitHub repo) (Winner of 2022 Rothman Prize)

  • Comparing bicyclists who use smartphone apps to record rides with those who do not: Implications for representativeness and selection bias
    Michael Garber, Kari Watkins, Michael Kramer
    Journal of Transport & Health. 2019. (full text in PubMed Central)

  • Sampling and Sampling Frames in Big Data Epidemiology
    Steve Mooney, Michael Garber
    Current Epidemiology Reports. 2019. (full text in PubMed Central)

Causal-inference concepts

At Emory, I was a teaching assistant in a course covering methods for causal inference in observational data. Informed by this experience, I have written about how the potential-outcome model from epidemiology can be a useful frame for electoral questions and have commented on the utility of causal-inference tools for environmental epidemiology:

  • A definition of the causal effect of a political party’s nominee on the U.S. general presidential election using counterfactual response types
    Michael Garber, Lindsay Collin, Dana Flanders
    Annals of Epidemiology. 2020. (full text in PubMed Central)

  • Is the Smog Lifting? Causal Inference in Environmental Epidemiology
    Dana Flanders, Michael Garber
    Epidemiology. 2019. (full text above and in PubMed Central)

  • The Authors Respond
    Dana Flanders, Michael Garber
    Epidemiology. 2019. (full text above and in PubMed Central)
